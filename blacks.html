Black Saturday

Date: Varies (Saturday before Easter)

Overview

Black Saturday, also known as Holy Saturday, is the day between Good Friday and Easter Sunday in the Christian liturgical calendar. It commemorates the day that Jesus Christ's body lay in the tomb after his crucifixion and before his resurrection. This day is often marked by somber reflection and anticipation of the joyous celebration of Easter. Many Christian traditions observe Black Saturday with quiet contemplation, prayer, and sometimes fasting. In some cultures, it is a time for preparing for the Easter Vigil, which is held on the night of Holy Saturday and celebrates the resurrection of Jesus. The day symbolizes a period of waiting and hope, reflecting on themes of death and renewal.
