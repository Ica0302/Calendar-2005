All Saint's Day

Date: November 1

Overview

All Saint's Day, celebrated on November 1st, is a Christian holiday dedicated to honoring all saints, known and unknown. It is a day to remember and celebrate the lives of those who have attained sainthood and are believed to be in heaven. The holiday has its roots in the early Christian tradition of commemorating martyrs and saints, and it was officially established by the Church in the 7th century. All Saint's Day is often marked by attending church services, lighting candles, and visiting cemeteries to pay respects to deceased loved ones. In many cultures, it is a time for reflection on the virtues and sacrifices of saints, as well as a celebration of faith and community.
