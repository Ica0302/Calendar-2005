Valentine's Day

February 14th

Overview

Valentine's Day, celebrated on February 14th, is a day dedicated to expressing love and affection between intimate partners, friends, and family. The holiday has its origins in both Christian and ancient Roman traditions, particularly associated with Saint Valentine, a martyr who was known for performing weddings for soldiers forbidden to marry. Over time, Valentine's Day has evolved into a widely recognized celebration of romantic love, characterized by the exchange of cards, flowers (especially roses), chocolates, and other gifts. It is a popular occasion for couples to spend quality time together and for individuals to show appreciation for their loved ones.
