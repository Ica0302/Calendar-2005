Labor's Day/h1>

Date: First Monday in September

Overview

Labor Day, celebrated on the first Monday in September, is a public holiday dedicated to honoring the contributions and achievements of workers and the labor movement. It originated in the late 19th century during the Industrial Revolution, a time when labor unions were advocating for better working conditions, fair wages, and reasonable working hours. Labor Day serves as a reminder of the importance of workers in society and their role in building and sustaining economies. The holiday is often marked by parades, picnics, barbecues, and various recreational activities, providing a well-deserved break for workers and their families. It also signifies the unofficial end of summer in many countries, making it a popular time for outdoor gatherings and celebrations.
